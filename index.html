Research Interests
My primary research interests lie in the automatic synthesis and verification of programs through formal methods, and the mathematical theory that underlies these formal methods. For example, my work in POPL21, CAV21, and OOPSLA24 explore various new approaches to program synthesis, while those in POPL23 and PLDI23 focus on establishing theoretical foundations to previously challenging problems in the field.

I also hold an interest in lower-level systems and security research, in particular the intersection between these fields and formal methods. For example, my work in POPL25 helps bring integer-pointer casting in C, an important but surprisingly complex low-level feature, to the world of formal verification.
